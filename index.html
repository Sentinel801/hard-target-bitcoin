Start here

A practical place to begin.

Most Bitcoin security advice focuses on technical compromise: malware, phishing, exchange failures, and device hygiene. Those risks matter. But a different category of risk appears when a person is known, visible, and thought to control meaningful value. That is where privacy, routines, household planning, and custody design begin to matter in a different way.

If you are new here, do not start by buying more gear or copying someone else’s setup. Start by understanding your own threat model. Then reduce the easy leaks, remove obvious weak points, and build habits that make rushed compliance harder and careless exposure less likely.

1

Learn the threat

Learn why coercion risk is different from technical theft and why self-custody changes the consequences of visibility.

2

Reduce visibility

Audit your online presence, public routines, identity leakage, and the small signals that can compound into targeting risk.

3

Rethink custody

Re-examine convenience, key storage, recovery materials, and fast-access paths through a coercion lens rather than a purely technical one.

4

Practice habits

Build repeatable habits for home life, travel, communication, and family awareness so your security is not dependent on improvisation.

Core principles

A simple framework for becoming a harder target.

Principle 01

Privacy is the first layer.

Privacy is not paranoia. It is the disciplined reduction of unnecessary signals. The less others know about your holdings, routines, address, travel, and setup, the less useful you are as a target.

Principle 02

Visibility creates risk.

Visibility compounds. A conference badge, a public bio, a podcast mention, a real-time post, and a known city can be enough to give the wrong person a starting point.

Principle 03

Fast access can increase danger.

Convenience has a cost. If meaningful value can be accessed quickly by one person under stress, then the system may be optimized for speed at exactly the moment when delay would matter most.

Principle 04

Security is a system.

No wallet exists in isolation. Devices, backups, routines, travel behavior, household awareness, and communications all interact. Real security comes from how those parts work together under pressure.

Glossary

Key terms for the site’s framework.

Wrench attack
A coercive theft scenario where an attacker tries to force access, disclosure, or compliance through threat or violence rather than technical compromise.
OPSEC
Operational security: the habits, routines, and communication choices that reduce what outsiders can learn, infer, or exploit about you.
Doxxing
The exposure or linking of personal identifying information in a way that can make someone easier to locate, profile, or target.
Threat model
A practical description of what you are protecting, from whom, by what means, and under what kinds of pressure or constraints.

Built by someone who understands criminal risk.

I spent 21 years working as a prosecutor and federal agent. Hard Target Bitcoin is where I translate that experience into educational material for people who want to think more clearly about criminal targeting, avoidable exposure, and the practical decisions that make someone harder to exploit.

This is an education-first project. The purpose is to publish useful guidance, build a body of serious work, and help readers improve judgment before this site expands into anything broader. No hype, no fear tactics, and no performance of expertise—just clear thinking applied to a real problem.
